
STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE: SOP-102
TITLE: ELECTRONIC SIGNATURES AND SIGNING AUTHORITY
VERSION: 1

1.0 PURPOSE
This procedure governs the definition, application, and verification of electronic signatures to ensure they are the legally binding equivalent of traditional handwritten signatures.

2.0 SCOPE
Applies to all electronic signatures executed on standard operating procedures, validation plans, clinical reports, and batch release records.

3.0 ELECTRONIC SIGNATURE REQUIREMENTS
3.1 Dual-Factor Signature: The execution of an electronic signature requires the input of two distinct identification components: a unique user ID and password, or a biometric identifier combined with password authentication.
3.2 Signature Manifestation: Every electronically signed document must visually display the following three signature components:
  - The printed name of the signer.
  - The date and time stamp (including time zone) when the signature was executed.
  - The meaning associated with the signature (e.g., review, approval, authorship, or responsibility).
3.3 Non-Repudiation: Electronic signatures shall be unique to one individual and shall not be copied, reassigned, or transferred. No two individuals shall share signing credentials.
